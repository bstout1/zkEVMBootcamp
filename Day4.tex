\begin{prb}\label{P1}
Working with the following set of integers $S=\{ 0,1,2,3,4,5,6\}$, compute the following: $4+4$, $3*5$, $3^{-1}$
\end{prb}

$S$ is the finite field of seven elements, or $\FF_7$. Therefore $4+4=8=1\textrm{ (mod 7)}$, $3*5=15=1\textrm{ (mod 7)}$ which also shows that $3^{-1}=5\textrm{ (mod 7)}$.

\begin{prb}\label{P2}
For $S=\{ 0,1,2,3,4,5,6\}$ can we consider $S$ and the operationg $+$ to be a group?
\end{prb}

Yes, because $S=\FF_7$ we know that $S$ is a group under addition and the non-zero elements form a group under multiplication.

\begin{prb}\label{P3}
What is $-13\textrm{ (mod 5)}$?
\end{prb}
Adding multiples of the modulus we obtain $-13 = -13 + 15 = 2\textrm{ (mod 5)}$.

\begin{prb}\label{P4}
For the polynomial $p(x)=x^3-x^2+4x-12$ find the positive root $a$. What is the degree of this polynomial?
\end{prb}

The degree of the polynomial is $3$, the largest power of $x$. A positive root for this polynomial is $x=2$ because $2^3-2^2+4*2-12=8-4+8-12=0$. Therefore the polynomial factors as $(x-2)(x^2+x+6)$.
